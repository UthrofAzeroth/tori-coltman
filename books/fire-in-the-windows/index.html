Fire in the Windows

The Last Bright Things Book 1: a tender New York romance about shelter, belonging, and the people who let us in.

Fire in the Windows opens The Last Bright Things with a tender, intimate New York love story about shelter, belonging, and the people who let us in.

On a storm-soaked night, photographer Iris Wren steps into The Calder expecting another room to shoot and another evening to pass through unnoticed. Instead, a leak in the ceiling throws the bar into motion, and Iris finds herself shoulder to shoulder with Eli Mercer — a man whose steadiness is harder to walk away from than either of them expects.

The Calder is more than a bar. It is a refuge, a workplace, a witness, and the fragile centre of a small world held together by regulars, staff, old loyalties, and practical acts of care. As Iris keeps returning, attraction deepens into something riskier: the possibility of being known, wanted, and allowed to belong.
