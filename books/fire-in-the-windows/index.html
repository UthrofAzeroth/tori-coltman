Fire in the Windows

Tender, intimate, and full of city-night atmosphere, Fire in the Windows is a contemporary love story about shelter, belonging, and the people who let us in.

On a storm-soaked night in New York, photographer Iris Wren steps into The Calder expecting nothing more than another room to shoot and another evening to pass through unnoticed. Instead, a leak in the ceiling throws the whole bar into motion, and Iris finds herself shoulder to shoulder with Eli Mercer — a man who fixes things with quiet competence, dry humour, and a steadiness that is harder to walk away from than either of them expects.

The Calder is more than a bar. It is a refuge, a workplace, a witness, and the fragile centre of a small world held together by regulars, staff, old loyalties, and the kind of care people rarely name out loud. As Iris keeps returning, what begins as attraction deepens into something riskier: the possibility of being known, of being wanted, and of belonging somewhere for real.

Fire in the Windows is a love story about the places that shelter us, the people who let us in, and the light that stays visible even when everything outside goes dark.
