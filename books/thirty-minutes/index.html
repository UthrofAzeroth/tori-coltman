Thirty Minutes

A contemporary love story about attraction, hesitation, and the way a brief encounter can turn into something far more difficult to ignore.

Set in the recognisable world of ordinary routines and chance meetings, Thirty Minutes is interested in chemistry, restraint, and the fragile point at which a short encounter stops feeling small and starts to matter.

The novel stays close to emotional uncertainty: what people say, what they hold back, and the speed with which a life can tilt when connection arrives at exactly the wrong or right moment.
