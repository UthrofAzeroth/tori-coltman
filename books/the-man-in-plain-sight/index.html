The Man in Plain Sight

A contemporary romance about desire, inheritance, social control, and the cost of choosing what is true over what is easy.

The Man in Plain Sight is a contemporary romance about attraction, power, inheritance, and the private cost of living inside a world built on appearances.

Set against old money, social pressure, and the expectations that travel through family and class, the novel follows a connection that becomes difficult to deny. It is a story about choosing what is true over what is easy, and about the people who become visible only when someone finally looks closely enough.
